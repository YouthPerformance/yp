# Voice Editing Guide for Content Team

> Write articles by speaking. No typing required.

---

## Quick Start (30 seconds)

1. **Open Dashboard**: `/admin/content-strategy`
2. **Click an Article**: Tap any article card to open the editor
3. **Expand a Section**: Tap the section you want to write
4. **Tap the Mic**: Hold to record, release to transcribe
5. **Review & Save**: Edit if needed, tap "Save"

## Voice Recording Tips

### Do This âœ“

- **Speak in complete thoughts**
  > "Speed training for youth athletes isn't about running faster. It's about moving smarter. The fastest kids aren't always the ones with the best genetics. They're the ones who've learned to use their body efficiently."

- **Use YP brand language**
  > "We call this building your chassis. A strong chassis means stable joints, reactive muscles, and the ability to explode in any direction."

- **Include specific details**
  > "This drill takes about 45 seconds. Athletes should focus on quick foot contacts, keeping ground time under 0.2 seconds."

### Avoid This âœ—

- Starting with "um" or "so"
- Long pauses mid-sentence
- Reading from a script (sounds robotic)
- Whispering or mumbling

## Section-by-Section Guide

### Introduction (ğŸ“)
What to say:
- Hook the reader with a bold statement
- State the problem clearly
- Preview what they'll learn

Example prompt to yourself:
> "If a parent asked me why speed training matters for their 10-year-old, what would I say?"

### Key Concepts (ğŸ“„)
What to say:
- The science/reasoning behind it
- Why this approach works
- Common misconceptions to address

### Drills (ğŸƒ)
What to include:
- Drill name
- Setup/equipment needed
- Step-by-step instructions
- Coaching cues (what to watch for)
- Duration and reps

Example:
> "A-Skip Progressions. No equipment needed. Start at one end of a 20-yard space. Skip forward, driving the knee up to hip height on each skip. Focus on staying tall, keeping your core engaged. Do 2 sets of 20 yards with 30 seconds rest between."

### FAQs (â“)
Format:
> "Question: At what age should kids start speed training? Answer: Kids can start basic movement training as young as 6, but structured speed work typically begins around 8-10..."

### Call to Action (ğŸ¯)
What to say:
- Summarize the key takeaway
- Tell them what to do next
- Link to related content

## Troubleshooting

**Mic not working?**
- Check browser permissions (click lock icon in URL bar)
- Try refreshing the page
- Make sure no other app is using the mic

**Transcription is wrong?**
- Speak closer to the mic
- Reduce background noise
- Speak more slowly and clearly

**Text not saving?**
- Check your internet connection
- Look for the "Last saved" timestamp
- Try clicking Save manually
